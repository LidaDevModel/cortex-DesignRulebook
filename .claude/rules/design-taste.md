# Design Taste Profile — Avante Cortex

## Aesthetic Direction
- Gravitates toward: minimal and precise, luxury and refined
- Avoids: visual noise and competing elements, interfaces that feel heavy or intimidating to new users
- Products admired: Duolingo (complexity made accessible, clear hierarchy, cognitive load reduction), Spotify (large content balanced with intuitive navigation, serves both goal-oriented and exploratory users), Notion (flexible, scalable, consistent design system across content types)
- Products disliked: TikTok (too many elements competing for attention, overwhelming visual density), Jira (visually heavy, intimidating for new users)

## Color
- Default mode: both dark and light equally — neither is an afterthought
- Accent approach: restrained and singular — one accent, used with intention
- Temperature: cool neutrals with authority; no gradients for decoration

## Typography
- Primary font: Plus Jakarta Sans — geometric, modern, legible across sizes and weights
- Philosophy: font selection driven by accessibility, readability, language support, and system-wide scalability — not personality for its own sake
- Fonts acceptable: Inter, SF Pro, IBM Plex Sans as fallbacks or secondary roles
- Fonts to avoid: decorative or heavily stylised display fonts in UI contexts — credibility and readability over personality

## Density & Space
- Default density: spacious — generous layouts, room to think
- Whitespace philosophy: intentional and earned; breathing room is part of the information design, not a luxury
- Exception: dashboards and data views may be denser where information density serves the user's task

## Motion
- Philosophy: purposeful moments — transitions where they serve comprehension
- Acceptable motion: page transitions, state changes, loading feedback — all under 250ms, eased naturally
- Never: bounce, elastic easing, decorative animations, motion that delays task completion

## The Signature Detail
- What separates good from exceptional: anticipating the user before they encounter the problem
- Specifically: thoughtful empty states, helpful error messages, clear action feedback, consistent spacing, edge cases handled — loading, validation, onboarding, error recovery
- Standard: every design decision supports clarity, usability, or trust — nothing decorative without function

## Cortex-Specific Notes
- Users range from field security guards (mobile, high-stress, low time) to ops managers (desktop, analytical, data-focused) — design must serve both ends of that spectrum
- New hires are a primary audience — the interface must feel welcoming and confidence-building, never intimidating
- The platform carries operational weight — tone is authoritative and precise, not playful
- Role-based access means the same product must feel appropriately scoped for each user — no cognitive overhead from irrelevant content
